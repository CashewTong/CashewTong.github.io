Rhetorical Devices

Red Herring
   • misleading; a distraction from a real issue
Straw Man
    •intentionally misrepresenting an opponent's real argument because the original is hard to defeat
False Equivalency
    •believing two things should be given equal weight in your favour, but when they are totally not equivalent

Human Nature

Definition: the general psychological characteristics, feelings, and behavioral traits of humankind, regarded as shared by all humans.
Theories of Human Nature:
    •The Blank Slate    "Empiricism"
    •The Noble Savage    "Romanticism"
    •The Ghost in the Machine    "Dualism"

Next

The Blank Slate

Philosopher: John Locke
• Also known as "Tabula Rasa" in Latin
• States everyone is born as a "blank slate" and through civilization and experiences, people begin to learn to process data to become who they truly are.
• Most reasonable and logical theory
• People are raised by people around them, their parents, culture and they become who they are because of it.

Next

The Noble Savage

Philosopher: Jean-Jacques Rousseau
• Says everyone is born with an innate goodness but through civilization corrupts them from their nature self.
• Most optimisitc theory out of the three
• Civilization brings privileges into our society which corrupts us as humans. Wars and conflicts are created because of some of the things below.
    • Money - $$$
    • Power - Fighting for high class
    • Land - Nations vs. Nations for territory
    • Technology - & its advancement

Next

The Ghost in the Machine

Philosopher: Gilbert Ryles & Rene Descartes
• Interesting Fact: It was not Rene Descartes who wrote the Ghost in the Machine theory but instead Gilbert Ryles in his book called The Concept of Mind. Ryles based off his theory from Descartes' mind-body dualism
• The fundamental relationship or distinction between mind and body
• Believed the mind is unlimited and the body is limited
• The mind can live without the body but the body cannot live without the mind
• Implicitly religious
• Differences:
    • "I have a body" & "I am a body"
    • "Mind vs Brain?"
